## Syllabus

- **Introduction of Analytical Chemistry**: qualitative analysis and quantitative analysis
- **Experimental errors** (systematic and random): Estimation of experimental errors, normal distribution, estimation of mean and error from a finite number of measurements. Standard deviation of measurements and standard deviation of the mean.
- **Propagation of errors**: The development of the general formula
- **Linear regression**: Development of the equations and discussing the assumptions, calibration methods, external standard, standard addition, internal standard
- **pH of acids and bases**: Exact mathematical description and approximations; Buffer solution, general Brønsted equation, exact mathematical description and application of chemical approximations
- **Titrations**: Mathematical description titration curve, precipitation, acid-bases, precipitation and redox titrations. Calculation and discussion of the titration accuracy, the methods to find end point by first derivative and Gran plot, Potentiometry
- **Electroanalysis**: Nernst equation, Voltammetry, Coulometry and Electrogravimetry
- **Gravimetric analysis**: Precipitation process and factors affecting the precipitation nature and its purity

## 1. Introduction of Analytical Chemistry (A.C.)

### 1.1 What is Anal. Chem.?

Anal. Chem. is one of most important branch of Chemistry.

> **Definition from FECS:** Analytical Chemistry is a scientific discipline that develops and applies methods, instruments and strategies to obtain information on the composition and nature of matter in space and time.
>
> FECS = Federation of European Chemical Societies

**A-Chem. is an Information Science.**

### 1.5 What is Anal. Chem. to do?

**A-Chem. is the science to find the composition of matters.**

1. **Qualitative Analysis** - what is it?
2. **Quantitative Analysis** - how much is it?
3. **Structure Analysis**
4. **Property Characterization**
5. **Spatial Analysis** - mapping technology, Distribution of analyte through sample
6. **Time-dependent Analysis** - Change of analyte/property over time

### 1.6 General terms in "A-Chem. Talk"

**Method related terms:**
- **Analytical technique** - an approach to the assay which involves:
  - Determining what question is being asked
  - Using an appropriate sample
  - Sample preparation
  - Gathering measurements
  - Reporting results

**Sample related terms:**
- **Sample** - representative of the tested material
- **Matrix** - all substance in the sample except the target
- **Analyte** - specific substance (target) we measure
  - Major: > 1%
  - Minor: 0.01% to 1%
  - Trace: below 0.01%

### 1.7 General steps in Anal. Chem.

1. **Sampling** - representative?
2. **Sample preparation** - interference?
3. **Analytical method** - reliable?

**Uncertainty:** The uncertainty of a measurement is as important as measurement itself in Analytical Science.

**Sample preparation steps:**
- Dissolving
- Extraction
- Concentration
- Masking (interference not detected)

**Analytical quality management (AQM):**
- Construct different samples for analysis
- Repeat measurements
- Use several analytical methods
- Measuring by different people
