H12-1. According to its electric double layer structure, choose the best washing liquid 
for the precipitate AgCl, which grows in a solution HCl with excess of AgNO3. 
a. H2O (boiling point: 100 °C)        
b. NH4NO3 (boiling point: 210 °C)  
c. (NH4)2CO3 (boiling point: 336 °C)  
       d. KNO3 (boiling point: 400 °C)         
b. NH4NO3  
 
Firstly, it is better to choose the electrolyte of an ion keep identical to the 2nd ionic 
layer of target precipitate. If H2O is chosen as the washing liquid, the 2nd ionic layer is 
diluted. The charged solid particles repel one another and the precipitate breaks up---
peptization
. 
According to this rule b and d can be selected  
And NH4NO3 (boiling point: 210 °C) is better than KNO3 (boiling point: 400 °C), 
because the NH4+ is easy to be converted into NH3 under low temperature.
