H7-1. Which statement is true (single choice question)?  
a. The equivalence point occurs at a more acidic as Ka decreases in acid-base 
titrations.  
b. The titration error decreases as Ka and the reactant’s concentrations decrease in 
acid-base titrations. 
c. As a general rule, one should select an indicator that changes color at the pH 
approximately to the titration equivalence point. 
d. The buffer capacity () depends upon its concentration; and buffer pH is 
dependent of the solution volume. 
 
Answer: c 
a. Acid-base titration involves neutralization reactions, in which an acid (or 
base) reacts with an equivalent amount of a base (or acid).  The pH of titrated 
solution changes with the successive amounts of the titrant added. At the point 
of completion of the neutralization reaction (the equivalence point), the pH 
depends on the final products. The equivalence point occurs at a more basic 
pH as Ka is decreased in acid-base titration, which is due to the conjugate base 
formed. The pH of the conjugate base increases reciprocally in relation to the 
value of Ka. 
b. The titration error increases with a smaller Ka and lower reactant’s 
concentrations in acid-base titration. It is not practical to titrate an acid or base 
when its strength is too weak or its concentration is too dilute, whose titration 
break (titration scope) becomes too shallow to detect. 
d. The pH of a buffer is depend on its concentration is correct. The buffer 
capacity  would increase with the concentration. However, the pH is nearly 
irrelevant/ independent of the solution volume, because the volume cancels in 
numerator and denominator of the log term in the Henderson equation.
